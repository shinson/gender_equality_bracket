Documentation

Data came from the following sources:

  • IPEDS Data Center:
    • Graduation rate by gender (2013)
    • Number of acceptances by gender (2013-2014)
    • Number of applicants by gender (2013-2014)
    • Number of applicants by gender (2013-2014)
    • Number of basketball scholarships by gender (2006)
    • Number of Postsecondary Awards in STEM by gender (2008-2009)
    • Undergraduate Enrollment by gender (2008-2009)
  • Scholarship Stats:
    • Roster size by gender (2014)

Equation

After compiling the data, rates of acceptance, basketball scholarships, and STEM awards were calculating according to the percentage of the total number of applicants, roster size, and undergraduate enrollment respectively. Admissions rates were calculated by dividing the number of acceptance by the number of applicants. Basketball scholarship rates were calculated by the number of scholarships by roster size. Finally, STEM graduation rates were calculated by number of STEM awards and the undergraduate enrollment.

To determine the equality score, these rates were subtracted between the male rate and female rate. The absolute value of these differences were then added together to make the score. Teams with higher score meant more inequality. Teams were disqualified from the competition if there was no data or like with the case of Harvard, where no basketball are given to players.
